Sebbi Lee

Neuroscientist. Student. Artist.

Hello there! I'm Sebbi, a 3rd year Neuroscience major at Georgia Tech. I love combining art with my knowledge of what motivates people, to try and improve community experiences with technology!

I am primarily motivated by a desire to increase accessibility of technology for those with disabilities, as many family members of mine suffer from varying levels of permanent disabilities. More personally, I have been suffering from a temporary disability which has caused a decreases in the range of motion of my wrist.

Current Work.

Research Assistant
January 2021 - Present

Working on researching regarding muscle spindle regeneration in rats and mice at the Cope Lab (located at Georgia Tech), and reports directly to Dr. Travis Rotterman and Dr. Timothy Cope. My responsibilities include using a cyrostat to cut muscle, completing immunohistochemistry for muscle spindle analysis, and analyzing electrophysiological data on rat muscles.

I got into this research due to my fascination of the regeneration of the central nervous system, specifically the spinal cord.

Art. Design.

I've been doing art for as long as I could hold a pencil to paper and fell in love with telling stories with them. Ever since, I've been creating things from paintings to Minecraft perler bead bees to stickers!
